Global attributes typically refer to characteristics or properties that are applicable across various contexts or domains. While the specific global attributes can vary depending on the context, here are five examples of commonly recognized global attributes:

  1. Universality: This attribute implies that something is applicable or relevant to all individuals or entities worldwide, without any restrictions or limitations.
  2. Interconnectedness: It refers to the notion that various components or elements are connected or related to each other in a broader global context, highlighting the interdependence of different parts of the world.
  3. Diversity: This attribute acknowledges the presence of a wide range of variations, differences, or variety in aspects such as cultures, languages, traditions, and perspectives globally.
  4. Sustainability: This attribute emphasizes the importance of using resources and implementing practices that promote long-term environmental, social, and economic well-being on a global scale.
  5. Equality: This attribute represents the principle of fairness and equal treatment for all individuals, regardless of their backgrounds, identities, or circumstances. It emphasizes the idea of providing equal opportunities and rights worldwide.
